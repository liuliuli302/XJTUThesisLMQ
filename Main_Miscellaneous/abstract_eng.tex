\begin{englishabstract}

    The rapid advancement of digital technologies has resulted in an overwhelming
    surge of video content, thereby creating an urgent demand for efficient
    video summarization techniques to manage and extract relevant information. Existing
    approaches are often overly complex and computationally intensive,
    highlighting the necessity for lightweight solutions that are more practical
    and user-friendly in real-world applications.

    To address these limitations, we propose a novel Training-Free approach that
    leverages Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) with a segment-wise similarity
    contrast mechanism. This framework utilizes the generalization strength of
    MLLMs to evaluate video frames, and further refines the assessment through cross-modal
    similarity between image and text features, significantly reducing the
    architectural complexity and computational cost of the model.

    Furthermore, our method, \textbf{T}raning-\textbf{F}ree \textbf{V}ideo
    \textbf{S}ummarization \textbf{N}etwork (\textbf{TFVSN}), first directly
    estimates the importance scores of video frame sequences using MLLMs; Second,
    it generates textual captions and corresponding text features through Blip2,
    which are then compared with visual features from video frames. The
    similarity results serve to enhance the final importance scoring. Our experimental
    results demonstrate that our method achieves performance comparable to unsupervised
    approaches, and even surpasses some supervised methods on certain datasets, which
    highlights the potential of Training-Free architectures for video-related tasks.

    \englishkeywordstype{Video summarization; Multimodal LLMs; Training-free; Cross-modal alignment; Semantic similarity}{Application Fundamentals}

\end{englishabstract}
